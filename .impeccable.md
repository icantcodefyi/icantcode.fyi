## Design Context

### Users
Portfolio visitors: recruiters, fellow developers, potential collaborators, and hackathon organizers. They arrive from Twitter/GitHub/LinkedIn links, scanning quickly on laptops and phones. The job to be done: understand who Aniruddh is, what he builds, and whether to reach out.

### Brand Personality
Warm, understated, curious. A self-taught 20-year-old developer from India who loves anime, builds AI tools, wins hackathons, and writes code for fun. The interface should feel like a quiet studio — personal artifacts arranged with care, not a corporate pitch deck.

### Aesthetic Direction
Soft pastel palette on a warm off-white ground. Light mode only. Think: a well-curated Notion page meets a Japanese stationery brand. Generous whitespace, asymmetric compositions, type-driven hierarchy. No dark mode glow, no gradients, no hero metrics. The masonry photo gallery at the bottom should feel like pinned polaroids — lived-in, not stock.

Anti-references: generic developer portfolios with cyan/purple dark themes, card grids with identical layouts, "I'm a Full Stack Developer" hero banners.

References: Dieter Rams's quiet order, Muji's restraint, the editorial calm of a well-set book page.

### Design Principles
1. **Quiet confidence** — let the work speak; avoid shouting
2. **Warmth over polish** — slightly imperfect, human, approachable
3. **Density without clutter** — show a lot of information in little space, but never feel cramped
4. **One memorable moment** — the masonry hackathon gallery should be the emotional anchor of the page
5. **Typography-first** — hierarchy comes from type scale and weight, not decoration
